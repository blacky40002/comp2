For a few moments he walked at my side in silence. Then he slipped his arm through mine, and pressing it gently with his hand, said softly and very earnestly: “I’m awfully sorry, Doc. It is frightfully hard luck for you, though it couldn’t have been much better even if--but it’s no use talking of that. I _am_ sorry, old chap. But still, you know, you ought to try to put it away. _She_ wouldn’t have wished you to make yourself unhappy about her.”
